The Problem

Drop-in Revenue Is Declining and Cost-Cutting Is Exhausted

FBC is a member-owned cooperative bouldering gym in Fredericton, NB (~85,000 people). Drop-in attendance, the gym's most profitable revenue category, has been falling. Staff is at bare minimum: one full-time ED, a part-time program director, paid coaches and setters. There is no room left to cut. Profitable programs (youth, adult coaching, camps, competitive teams) are running but insufficient to cover the shortfall. The 2025 consolidated net loss was $35,059.

This Report Asks

Can a cooperative bouldering gym recover financially by systematically re-engaging its existing contacts, converting more drop-ins to members, and leveraging its volunteer community?

Without significant new capital. Without adding paid staff. Without fundamentally changing the model.

Highest Impact

Make Volunteering Optional

Members volunteer for additional benefits (keycard access, voting rights, store credit) rather than as a condition of membership. This is the model at Collective Strength and the Minnesota Co-op. Voluntary participation produces more reliable volunteers and avoids DOL fair-labor exposure.

CBJ Minnesota Co-op DOL
Distribute Knowledge

Written ops manual, shared leadership roles, deliberate delegation. The gym must function without any single person. Rise Above

Member-Run Programming

League nights and social climbs run by members create recurring traffic at zero payroll. One willing volunteer, one whiteboard. CWA

Automate Admin

RGP + Zapier + Mailchimp removes repetitive follow-up from the ED and volunteers. ~$13-33/month. HARNESS
